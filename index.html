Frequently Asked Questions

Clinical research is a branch of medical science that involves studying health outcomes in human populations to improve the understanding of diseases, treatments, and interventions.

Clinical trials are divided into several phases:
Phase 1: Tests the safety and dosage of a drug.
Phase 2: Tests the efficacy and side effects.
Phase 3: Confirms effectiveness, monitors side effects, and compares to standard treatments.
Phase 4: Involves post-marketing surveillance to track long-term effects.

A Clinical Research Associate (CRA) is responsible for overseeing the clinical trial process, ensuring compliance with regulations, monitoring patient safety, and managing trial logistics, data, and documentation.

Good Clinical Practice (GCP) is an international ethical and scientific quality standard for designing, conducting, and reporting clinical trials. It ensures that trials are conducted with integrity, safety, and compliance to regulatory requirements.
